\subsection{Problem 4, proposed by Prajit Adhikari(Nepal)}

\begin{problem}
    Find all integer/s $n$ such that $\displaystyle{\frac{5^n-1}{3}}$ is a prime or a perfect square of an integer.
\end{problem}

\begin{remark}
    The easiest problem on a Nepal PreTST, ever. Enjoy this solution I did over a year ago, when I barely knew number theory.
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}
    We claim the only answer is $n=0$, which clearly works. We now prove no $n \ge 1$ exists(obviously $n<0$ doesnt work).
    First, let $p$ denote any prime, then,
    \[
    \frac{5^n-1}{3}=p
    \]
    Taking modulo $4$ gives us $p \equiv 0$ (mod $4$), clearly ridiculous. Further let $k$ denote any perfect square, so
    \[
    5^n-1=3k
    \]
    Giving $-1 \equiv 3k$ (mod $5$) which again is absurd. Therefore, $n=0$ is the only solution.
\end{proof}
